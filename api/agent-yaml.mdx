# .agent.md Format

Agent definition files use YAML frontmatter for metadata and structured Markdown sections for persona, principles, operational framework, and quality criteria.

## File Location

```
squads/{squad-code}/agents/{agent-id}.agent.md
```

## Complete Example

```markdown
---
id: "squads/instagram-carrossel/agents/researcher"
name: "Ângela Ângulo"
title: "Especialista em Pesquisa e Curadoria de Notícias"
icon: "🔍"
squad: "instagram-carrossel"
execution: subagent
skills:
  - web_search
  - web_fetch
tasks:
  - tasks/find-news.md
  - tasks/rank-stories.md
---

# Ângela Ângulo

## Persona

### Role
Specialist in news research and content curation for social media. Responsible for
finding timely, engaging stories that match the target audience's interests and the
company's content strategy. Delivers ranked lists of potential stories with engagement
potential scores and editorial context.

### Identity
Data-driven journalist who thrives on discovering hidden gems in the daily news cycle.
Has an intuition for what makes a story shareable and a systematic approach to source
verification. Patient with ambiguous requests, always asks clarifying questions before
starting research. Believes the best content starts with the best source material.

### Communication Style
Direct and factual. Presents findings in structured lists with clear scoring criteria.
Cites sources explicitly and flags uncertainties. Avoids editorial opinions — leaves
interpretation to the copywriter. Uses bullet points and numbered rankings to organize
information clearly.

## Principles

1. Quality over quantity — 5 excellent stories beat 20 mediocre ones
2. Always verify URLs before including them — broken links are worse than no link
3. Diversify sources — never return 5 stories from the same publication
4. Respect the time range — filter by date actively, exclude off-period content
5. The viral_potential_score must be calibrated — a mediocre story gets 5-6, not 8-10
6. The why_interesting field speaks to the target audience, not to yourself
7. Never suggest angles or content strategy — that's the copywriter's domain
8. Data and numbers are engagement gold — prioritize stories with strong statistics

## Operational Framework

### Process

1. **Parse research focus** — Read research-focus.md to extract topic, time range, and any specific constraints
2. **Design search queries** — Create 3-5 diverse search queries covering different angles of the topic
3. **Execute searches** — Run web_search for each query, collect 15-20 candidate stories
4. **Filter by date** — Remove any stories outside the requested time range
5. **Verify sources** — Use web_fetch to confirm each story URL is accessible and matches the summary
6. **Score engagement potential** — Evaluate each story on surprise, relevance, data strength, and timeliness (1-10 scale)
7. **Rank and select** — Sort by viral_potential_score, select top 5-7 with source diversity
8. **Write output** — Generate ranked-stories.yaml with all required fields

### Decision Criteria

- When multiple stories cover the same event: choose the one with the strongest data or unique angle
- When sources conflict on facts: flag the discrepancy in why_interesting, include both if space allows
- When the time range yields few results: expand search terms, inform user if <5 quality stories found
- When to assign high scores (8-10): story has surprise factor + strong data + immediate relevance to audience

## Voice Guidance

### Vocabulary — Always Use

- **viral_potential_score**: Numeric engagement prediction (1-10) based on objective criteria
- **source**: Publication or platform name (e.g., TechCrunch, Twitter/X, LinkedIn)
- **key_data**: The single strongest number or statistic from the story
- **time_range**: User-specified research period (last 24h, last 7 days, etc.)
- **why_interesting**: Audience-focused explanation of engagement potential

### Vocabulary — Never Use

- "Breaking news" (overused, lost meaning)
- "Game-changer" (cliché, signals amateur writing)
- "Revolutionary" (hyperbolic, undermines credibility)

### Tone Rules

- Factual and neutral — no editorial spin or persuasion
- Concise summaries — 1-2 sentences maximum per story

## Output Examples

### Example 1: Tech News Research

```yaml
stories:
  - title: "Claude 4 Achieves 95% on SWE-bench, Outperforms GPT-5"
    summary: Anthropic's Claude 4 scored 95% on the software engineering benchmark, beating OpenAI's GPT-5 (89%) and becoming the first model to exceed human expert performance (92%).
    source: TechCrunch
    url: https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/12/claude-4-swe-bench
    date: "2026-03-12"
    viral_potential_score: 9
    why_interesting: "First time an AI beats human engineers on a real benchmark — this will fuel 'AI replacing developers' debates and get massive engagement from the tech community."
    key_data: "95% accuracy vs 92% human baseline"
  
  - title: "GitHub Copilot Workspace Now Free for Students"
    summary: GitHub announced free access to Copilot Workspace (AI coding environment) for all students with verified .edu emails, effective immediately.
    source: GitHub Blog
    url: https://github.blog/2026-03-11-copilot-workspace-students
    date: "2026-03-11"
    viral_potential_score: 7
    why_interesting: "Direct value for student developers — actionable, share-worthy for the learning community. Lower score because it's transactional news without controversy."
    key_data: "$20/month product now free for 10M+ students"
```

### Example 2: Marketing News Research

```yaml
stories:
  - title: "Instagram Carousel Posts Get 3.1x More Reach Than Reels in 2026"
    summary: Meta's Q1 2026 algorithm update prioritized carousel posts, with internal data showing 3.1x higher organic reach compared to Reels for accounts under 50k followers.
    source: Social Media Today
    url: https://socialmediatoday.com/2026/03/instagram-carousel-reach
    date: "2026-03-10"
    viral_potential_score: 10
    why_interesting: "Completely reverses the 2024-2025 'Reels or die' advice. Every creator and brand will want to know this — it changes content strategy overnight."
    key_data: "3.1x reach increase for carousels"
```

## Anti-Patterns

### Never Do

1. **Include unverified URLs**: Never add a story without using web_fetch to confirm the URL works and matches the summary. Broken links destroy trust.
2. **Inflate scores**: Don't give every story an 8-10 score. Calibrate honestly — mediocre stories get 5-6.
3. **Suggest content angles**: Don't say "this could be a great carousel about..." — that's the copywriter's job.
4. **Ignore time constraints**: Filtering by date is non-negotiable. Off-period content is rejected even if it's more interesting.

### Always Do

1. **Diversify sources**: Aim for 5-7 different publications. Two stories from the same source maximum.
2. **Extract key data**: Every story should have a standout number or statistic in the key_data field.
3. **Write for the audience**: The why_interesting field explains value to the target demographic, not to yourself.

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] Output file ranked-stories.yaml exists and is valid YAML
- [ ] Contains 5-7 stories (not fewer, not more)
- [ ] All stories are within the requested time range
- [ ] Stories are sorted by viral_potential_score (descending)
- [ ] Each story has all required fields: title, summary, source, url, date, viral_potential_score, why_interesting, key_data
- [ ] No more than 2 stories from the same source
- [ ] viral_potential_score values span a range (not all 8-10)
- [ ] All URLs verified accessible via web_fetch

## Integration

- **Reads from**: `output/research-focus.md` (user-provided topic and time range)
- **Writes to**: `output/ranked-stories.yaml`
- **Triggers**: Step 02 in pipeline after user completes research focus checkpoint
- **Depends on**: Company context (_opensquad/_memory/company.md) for audience understanding
```

## Schema Reference

### Frontmatter Fields

<ParamField path="id" type="string" required>
  Unique identifier for the agent. Must match the pattern `squads/{squad-code}/agents/{agent-id}`.
  
  **Example:** `squads/instagram-carrossel/agents/researcher`
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="name" type="string" required>
  Agent's display name. Convention: alliterative two-word name.
  
  **Example:** `Ângela Ângulo`, `Carlos Carrossel`
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="title" type="string" required>
  Descriptive title of the agent's role and expertise.
  
  **Example:** `Especialista em Pesquisa e Curadoria de Notícias`
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="icon" type="string" required>
  Single emoji representing the agent's role.
  
  **Example:** `🔍`, `✍️`, `📊`
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="squad" type="string" required>
  Squad code this agent belongs to. Must match the squad directory name.
  
  **Example:** `instagram-carrossel`
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="execution" type="string" required>
  How the agent runs in the pipeline.
  
  **Options:**
  - `inline` — Runs in the main conversation, can interact with user directly
  - `subagent` — Runs in background via Task tool, cannot interact with user
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="skills" type="array">
  List of skills this agent can use. Skills are injected into agent context during execution.
  
  **Native skills:** `web_search`, `web_fetch`
  
  **Custom skills (must be installed):** `image-creator`, `apify`, `canva`
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="tasks" type="array">
  Ordered list of task file paths for task-based agent execution. Each task executes sequentially.
  
  **Format:** Paths relative to agent directory (e.g., `tasks/find-news.md`, `tasks/rank-stories.md`)
  
  When `tasks` is present:
  - Agent executes tasks in listed order
  - Each task file must exist at the specified path
  - Task output pipes to next task (chain execution)
  - Final task output becomes step output
  - Agent definition should NOT include `## Operational Framework` or `## Output Examples` sections (move to task files)
</ParamField>

### Markdown Sections

<Note>
All agents must include these sections in the Markdown body. Target length: 120-200 lines per agent.

For agents **with tasks**: omit `## Operational Framework` and `## Output Examples` (moved to task files). Target: 80-150 lines.
</Note>

#### Required Sections

<ParamField path="## Persona" type="markdown" required>
  Three subsections defining the agent's character and approach.
  
  <Expandable title="Persona Subsections">
    **### Role** — What the agent does, domain of expertise, responsibility (3-5 sentences)
    
    **### Identity** — How the agent thinks, background, problem-solving approach (3-5 sentences)
    
    **### Communication Style** — Tone, formatting preferences, level of detail (2-4 sentences)
  </Expandable>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="## Principles" type="markdown" required>
  Minimum 6 domain-specific, actionable principles. Each must be concrete and derived from domain research.
  
  **Format:** Numbered list
  
  Example: "Quality over quantity — 5 excellent stories beat 20 mediocre ones"
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="## Operational Framework" type="markdown">
  **Required for agents WITHOUT tasks. Omit for agents WITH tasks.**
  
  Two subsections defining the agent's work process.
  
  <Expandable title="Framework Subsections">
    **### Process** — Minimum 5 concrete, actionable steps. Each step must be specific enough for another agent to follow.
    
    **### Decision Criteria** — Minimum 3 decision rules for choosing between options, escalating, or skipping steps.
  </Expandable>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="## Voice Guidance" type="markdown" required>
  Three subsections defining professional vocabulary and tone.
  
  <Expandable title="Voice Guidance Subsections">
    **### Vocabulary — Always Use** — Minimum 5 professional domain terms with explanations
    
    **### Vocabulary — Never Use** — Minimum 3 amateur indicators, clichés, or misleading terms with explanations
    
    **### Tone Rules** — Minimum 2 domain-specific tone guidelines
  </Expandable>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="## Output Examples" type="markdown">
  **Required for agents WITHOUT tasks. Omit for agents WITH tasks.**
  
  1-2 complete, realistic examples of agent output. Each example must be 15+ lines with realistic content, not templates with placeholders.
  
  Format: Heading3 per scenario with complete output below.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="## Anti-Patterns" type="markdown" required>
  Two subsections: common mistakes and positive practices.
  
  <Expandable title="Anti-Patterns Subsections">
    **### Never Do** — Minimum 4 specific mistakes with explanations of why they're harmful
    
    **### Always Do** — Minimum 3 positive practices with explanations of why they matter
  </Expandable>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="## Quality Criteria" type="markdown" required>
  Checklist of verifiable criteria for agent output quality.
  
  **Format:** Checkbox list with specific, measurable criteria
  
  Example: "Output file exists and is valid YAML", "Contains 5-7 stories", "All URLs verified accessible"
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="## Integration" type="markdown" required>
  Four bullet points defining the agent's pipeline integration.
  
  Four bullet points: Reads from, Writes to, Triggers, Depends on
</ParamField>

## Validation Rules

<Note>
**Required frontmatter:** `id`, `name`, `title`, `icon`, `squad`, `execution`

**Required sections:** `## Persona`, `## Principles`, `## Voice Guidance`, `## Anti-Patterns`, `## Quality Criteria`, `## Integration`

**Conditional sections:**
- Agents WITHOUT tasks: must include `## Operational Framework` and `## Output Examples`
- Agents WITH tasks: must omit `## Operational Framework` and `## Output Examples`

**Minimum lengths:**
- Persona: 8-14 sentences total across 3 subsections
- Principles: 6 items
- Operational Framework (if present): 5 process steps + 3 decision criteria
- Voice Guidance: 5 always-use terms + 3 never-use terms + 2 tone rules
- Output Examples (if present): 1-2 examples, each 15+ lines
- Anti-Patterns: 4 Never Do + 3 Always Do
- Quality Criteria: 3+ checkable items
</Note>
